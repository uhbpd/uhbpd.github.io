Welcome to the Peritoneal Dialysis Service

The Peritoneal Dialysis (PD) service at University Hospitals Birmingham NHS Foundation Trust is delivered across Queen Elizabeth Hospital Birmingham (QEHB) and Birmingham Heartlands Hospital (BHH).

Our specialist multidisciplinary teams provide personalised support for people receiving peritoneal dialysis, helping patients manage their kidney care at home whilst maintaining independence and quality of life.

Whether you are exploring dialysis options, preparing to start treatment, or already receiving PD, our experienced team is here to support you every step of the way.

What is Peritoneal Dialysis?

Choosing a dialysis treatment is an important decision, and it’s natural to want an option that supports your independence, wellbeing, and day‑to‑day routine. 

Peritoneal dialysis uses the lining of your abdomen (the peritoneum) as a natural filter to clean your blood. A soft tube (catheter) allows dialysis fluid to move in and out of the abdomen, removing waste products and excess fluid.

Unlike hospital-based dialysis, PD is usually performed at home, giving many patients greater flexibility and control over treatment.

There are two different forms of PD - automated peritoneal dialysis (APD) and continuous ambulatory peritoneal dialysis (CAPD).

Why Choose Peritoneal Dialysis?

Peritoneal dialysis is a home-based treatment that offers many patients greater flexibility and independence whilst providing effective dialysis.


🏡 Home-Based Treatment

Carry out dialysis in the comfort of your own home and reduce the need for regular hospital visits.

⏰ Flexible Around Your Lifestyle

Choose treatment schedules that fit around your work, family life and daily routine.

🚗 Maintain Independence

Many patients value the freedom, confidence and control that PD provides.

✈️ Easier Travel

With planning and support, many patients can continue to travel whilst receiving treatment.

💙 Continuous Therapy

PD provides a gentle, continuous form of dialysis that works throughout the day or night.

👩‍⚕️ Specialist Support

Dedicated nurses, doctors and support staff are available to guide you throughout your treatment journey.

Support Every Step of the Way

Starting PD is a learning process, but you will not be on your own. Your PD team will provide:

  • Comprehensive training programme
  • Telephone advice and support
  • Home visits where required
  • Rapid access to specialist PD nurses
  • Ongoing reviews and education

Is Peritoneal Dialysis Right for You?

Every patient is different and the best treatment depends on your medical condition, lifestyle and personal preferences. PD may be particularly suitable if you:

  • Value independence and flexibility
  • Prefer treatment at home
  • Want to reduce hospital visits
  • Prefer to avoid needles during dialysis
